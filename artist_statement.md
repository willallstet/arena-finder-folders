Will Allstetter is an artist-researcher interested in the technological systems that quietly and profoundly define modern life, from the physical architecture of computer chips to the algorithms ferrying information on digital communication networks. His investment in these structures is not born out of a techno-optimist, progress-for-progress sake ethos, but the acknowledgement that they are the building blocks on which modern life is structured. In what ways do they seep into our analog, messy, human world? He pokes at the edges, reduces them to their component parts, and drudges up the elements big tech would prefer you ignore.

Rigorously tackling these topics means his work manifests in a variety of forms, from sculpture, to code, to essays, to performance, and more. It all drills down to this one question: in the (increasingly rapid) process of offloading human life onto digital domains, how do the structures of computational devices—hardware, software, design—alter experiences of reality?
