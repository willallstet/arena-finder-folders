Medienhaus
The Berlin University of the Arts’ building on Grunewaldstraße, bordering Kleistpark, in the district of Berlin-Schöneberg is commonly known as the Medienhaus (media house). Under its roof, you can find the study program Visual Communication (VK), the study program Art and Media (KuM), the Design program’s specialization Interactive Systems (ID5), and the specialization Audiovisual Communication of the study program Communication in Social and Economic Contexts (GWK).
The Institute of Transmedia Design (ITG) represents the interests of the Visual Communication program, while the members of the Institute of Time-Based Media (IZM) come from the study programs Art and Media, Visual Communication, Communication in Social and Economic Contexts, and Design.
With the Medienhaus, the UdK Berlin brings together all media-related specialized courses and disciplines within the College of Architecture, Media and Design, thus enabling a diverse network of connections between these areas and beyond the fields of traditional and digital media. During the “Rundgang – Open Days” of the UdK Berlin, the Medienhaus regularly showcases the entire spectrum of teaching and research.

Related Topics
The IZM is a focal point for media-related teaching and research at UdK Berlin. Teachers from several courses are located at the Medienhaus, thus making it possible for them to be connected and to exchange on an interdisciplinary level.
At the Institute of Transmedia Design complex processes of diverse topics will be analyzed and presented in a descriptive way.
In the course of study Visual Communication students deal with the planning, realisation and mediation of visual information in two or three dimensions.
The study program Art and Media is the only course at the UdK Berlin offering networked artistic teaching in the time-based media. Teachers of various subjects collaborate to offer this special study program, the focus being on interplay between artistic experiment and technical media.
