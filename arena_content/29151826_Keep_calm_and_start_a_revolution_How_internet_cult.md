In the middle of "Borne Out" (a play on French Prime Minister Elisabeth Borne's name); "16-64, it's a beer, not a career" (referring to the 1664 beer); and "If you force 64 on us, we'll force Mai 68 on you again" (a reference to May 1968, a period of significant civil unrest in France) Rachel's sign was quite unusual in the march against the pension reform on Tuesday, March 28, in Poitiers, western France. The 21-year-old woman wrote "It's not good for us," before sticking a photo of French YouTuber Squeezie, who has almost 18 million subscribers.
If the message itself isn't clear, the humorous side may have gone unnoticed by those who do not regularly follow one of the country's most-watched videographers and do not necessarily suspect that it is an answer to a question regularly asked in his videos.
