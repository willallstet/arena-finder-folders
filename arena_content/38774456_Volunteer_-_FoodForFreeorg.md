I AM A CURRENT VOLUNTEER
At Food For Free, our volunteers are at the heart of the work we do to fight food insecurity across Greater Boston. Whether you’re packing produce, building boxes, or bagging beans, every hour you spend with us helps feed a neighbor—and builds a more just, connected community.
current volunteer opportunities
Just Eats Program
Help us pack and build grocery boxes with fresh produce and pantry staples. These boxes are distributed through our hunger relief partners to families and individuals across Greater Boston who face barriers to accessing healthy food.
• Box packing: Work along an assembly line of volunteers to pack nutritious food into grocery boxes.• Box building: Fold and prepare cardboard boxes to be filled with healthy food.
School Markets Program
Support local school communities by packing dried beans from bulk into family-sized portions for distribution at our free pop-up markets on school grounds.
• Bean packing: A simple but essential task that ensures families have access to healthy proteins.
why work with food for free?
Direct Impact: Your time helps put fresh, healthy food into the hands of thousands of people each week.
Flexible Shifts: Most volunteer shifts are 2 hours long and are located at our Somerville Packing and Distribution Center. We occasionally will offer volunteer opportunities at nearby partner sites in Boston, Cambridge, and Somerville.
Great for Individuals or Small Groups: Whether you’re volunteering solo or with a friend or two, there’s always work to be done—and people to meet.
Be Part of a Movement: Join others committed to food justice and giving back to the community.
