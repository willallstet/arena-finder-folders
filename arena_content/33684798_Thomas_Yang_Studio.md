AtelierThomas Yang Studiothomas@yangsdesign.comInstagramI am a Taiwanese and Northern-Italian designer, with a background in industrial and object design. My craft is derived from culture, memory, and inherited techniques; it is based in a philosophy of care enacted to extend the life of the objects I make and offer respect to their living environments. Existing in the cross section of craft and design, my practice explores their intersection as a means of finding balance. My work has become a love letter to daily use, material, and deliberate detail through its exploration of hand-making methods.
As a new design studio, my current practice is one of uncovering the phenomena of making objects that become artifacts of the home. How do the completely ordinary objects of our everyday life become beautiful? My objects ask how we can learn of their meaning from our perception. My practice plays with the curiosity imbued by objects that pose questions, that pull you into their intentional details, and from afar become balanced in space.

Rock Table Lamp Dimension : 11"L x 9”W x  20" HMaterial : Cherry, Maple, River Stone
Lead Time : One of a KindPrice: Inquire
The Rocking Table Lamp continues the Ishibatate series in everyday objects. Using a hand-forged river stone from Trout Brook in combination with off-cut maple and cherry, it highlights lightness and weight through the species’ color variation. The design pushes the limitations of the material in both form and function. The thin stem supports an even thinner lampshade. The sheer veil allows the frame of the piece to remain visible, while the beeswax-laminated overcoat diffuses the light. Animated by a breeze or passing body, the shade dances.

Tian Den Piccola Dimension : 8″L x 9″W x 20″H Material : Yame Kozo Hadaura Paper, Bamboo String, Wood, White Ceramic.
Lead Time : 10 - 12 WeeksPrice: Inquire
The Tian Den lighting series is named after Taiwanese sky lanterns. A hand-sewn paper shade lays upon a structural wooden frame whose bamboo ribbing keeps the lantern’s form while allowing it to become animated by a breeze or passing body. The Yame Kozo Hadaura paper absorbs daylight, revealing the frame and accentuating the vitality of the lamp when both lit and unlit. Delicate details—wood wire coverings, an elliptical base, and handmade white stone ceramic fixtures—transform the mundane task of changing a light bulb into a ritual of care.

Ama Side Chair Dimension : 15″L x 16″W x 33″H Material : Wood
Lead Time : One of a KindPrice: Inquire
The Ama Chair is a delicate balance of opposing forces. The chair’s joinery pushes and pulls—with, and against—to create moments of stillness. Yang’s Ama, Chin Yin Wu, was an artist, a mother, a daughter, and a sister. Kind but stern, delicate but tough, thoughtful but clumsy, serious but funny; a study in balance. This chair is one interpretation of the designer’s grandmother, with many more to come.

Cabinet of Memories Dimension : 26″L x 14″W x 31″H Material : Wood
Lead Time : 16 - 18 WeeksPrice: Inquire
Built from the nostalgia for intersecting cultures, the Cabinet of Memories is based on the proportions of a traditional East-Asian cabinet. The cabinet’s devotion to daily objects is found in its reinterpreted design; book splines celebrate the craft of a publication’s spine while varying sizes of drawers and doors wrap the cabinet. Intended to hold space for long-term care of objects, the Cabinet of Memories is derived from recollections of Yang’s own family heirlooms.

Comet Rock BrushDimension : 3.5” L x2.75” W x 5” HMaterial : River stones from Trout Brook, Tampico Fiber Hay, and Beeswax.
Lead Time : One of a KindPrice: Inquire
Throughout childhood, my family collected stones and gifted them to one another. I was drawn to their formation and the role of water and time in honing each stone’s unique gravity and essence. These stone brushes are an ode to the active care required in making a home and the commitment to leave a space better than it was found. As a maker, a good broom is one of my most important tools, and these brushes transform the meditative act of sweeping from something mundane into something magical.

Lead Time : 10 -12 WeeksPrice: Inquire
This series of wall hooks is my first interpretation of the Shaker craft movement. Each one-off hook does not belong to a rigid system but rather is intended to highlight the existing architecture of a space. The surface of the mounting plate was made to resemble the studs that construct a wall. These hooks may stand alone or be brought together. They are part of an ongoing interpretation of wall-mounted objects.
